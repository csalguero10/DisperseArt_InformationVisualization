Research question:
How did architectural projects evolve in relation to institutional programming and audience engagement over time?

This question focuses on the temporal distribution of productions, the typology of works, and the spatial dimensions of the venues. It allows us to explore how architectural choices and staging practices respond to cultural, social, and economic changes.

We can combine information about seasons, productions, and people involved (directors, scenographers, architects) to reconstruct how the built environment of performance spaces is used, adapted, and reinterpreted over time.

The analysis can be visualised through timelines, network graphs linking spaces and productions, or maps of locations connected to architectural transformations.

Architecture – visualization placeholder

Research question:
How do infrastructural and organisational aspects shape the circulation of productions and collaborations?

This question highlights the “backstage” dimension of the institution: logistics, technical roles, and production credits that support each performance.

By modelling technical credits, production networks, and links between venues, we can observe patterns of collaboration and structural constraints over time.

Visualizations might include network diagrams of collaborations or Sankey diagrams of flows between seasons and production types.

Civil & Structural – visualization placeholder

Research question:
How do performances and productions relate to the wider urban and territorial context?

Here the focus is on places of première, touring circuits, and the relationship between the institution and the city or region in which it operates.

Linking datasets on venues, cities, and dates allows us to map trajectories and clusters of activity.

Visualizations can take the form of interactive maps or spatial-temporal heatmaps to reveal concentrations and gaps.

Landscape Design – visualization placeholder

Research question:
In what ways do cultural policies and institutional strategies emerge from the long-term programming of the theatre?

This question crosses seasons, genres, and audiences to look at how the institution positions itself within the cultural landscape of the city.

Combining season types, production sources, and entities involved makes it possible to trace strategic shifts and long-term orientations.

Visualizations might include stacked timelines or comparative plots of programming by category, season, or institutional partner.
